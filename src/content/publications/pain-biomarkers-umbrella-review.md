## Abstract

Pain and pain-related conditions remain a major global health and economic burden, and numerous biomarkers have been proposed to improve pain assessment, monitoring, and diagnosis — though their clinical utility remains unproven. This umbrella review systematically searched PubMed, Scopus, and the Cochrane Library through June 2025, screening systematic reviews and meta-analyses of clinical, biochemical, genetic, neurophysiological, and imaging biomarkers for pain, with methodological quality assessed using the AMSTAR-2 tool.

Across 49 included reviews (most rated low or critically low quality), inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6, TNF-α) showed the most consistent associations with chronic musculoskeletal pain, while neuroimaging and EEG measures reflected central nervous system changes. Proteomic multi-protein panels showed exploratory promise for fibromyalgia but lacked clinical validation, and evidence for genetic, hormonal, metabolic, and neurochemical markers remained inconsistent and methodologically limited.

No single biomarker has yet achieved clinical validation for chronic pain, though several biomarker classes show promise. Future work should prioritize high-quality longitudinal studies, standardized protocols, and multidimensional biomarker panels.
